The Solid State

1.1 General Characteristics of Solids

Solids have definite shape, mass, and volume. The constituent particles (atoms, molecules, or ions) are closely packed and have fixed positions. They exhibit strong intermolecular forces and possess minimum kinetic energy.

1.2 Classification of Solids

Based on the nature of order in particle arrangement, solids are classified as:

Crystalline Solids: Have a regular, repeating three-dimensional arrangement of particles. They have sharp melting points and anisotropic nature. Examples: NaCl, diamond, quartz.

Amorphous Solids: Have irregular arrangement of particles. They soften over a temperature range and are isotropic. Examples: Glass, rubber, plastics.

1.3 Classification of Crystalline Solids

Based on intermolecular forces:

• Ionic Solids: Held by electrostatic forces. Hard, brittle, high melting point. Example: NaCl, KCl
• Molecular Solids: Held by van der Waals forces. Soft, low melting point. Example: Ice, I₂
• Covalent Solids: Held by covalent bonds. Very hard, very high melting point. Example: Diamond, SiO₂
• Metallic Solids: Held by metallic bonds. Good conductors, malleable. Example: Fe, Cu, Ag

1.4 Crystal Lattice and Unit Cell

A crystal lattice is the regular three-dimensional arrangement of points in space. A unit cell is the smallest repeating unit that generates the entire lattice.

Types of unit cells: Primitive (simple), Body-centered, and Face-centered.

1.5 Number of Atoms in Unit Cell

• Primitive cubic: 1 atom per unit cell
• Body-centered cubic (bcc): 2 atoms per unit cell
• Face-centered cubic (fcc): 4 atoms per unit cell

1.6 Packing Efficiency

• Simple cubic: 52.4%
• Body-centered cubic: 68%
• Face-centered cubic ( ccp/hcp): 74%

1.7 Voids and Radius Ratio

In close packing, there are two types of voids:
• Tetrahedral voids: Coordination number 4, Number = 2N
• Octahedral voids: Coordination number 6, Number = N

1.8 Imperfections in Solids

Defects in crystals are of two types:

Point Defects: Stoichiometric defects (vacancy, interstitial), Non-stoichiometric defects (metal excess, metal deficiency), Impurity defects.

Line Defects: Edge dislocation and screw dislocation.

1.9 Electrical Properties

• Conductors: Conductivity 10⁴ to 10⁷ ohm⁻¹m⁻¹
• Semiconductors: Conductivity 10⁻⁶ to 10⁴ ohm⁻¹m⁻¹
• Insulators: Conductivity 10⁻²⁰ to 10⁻¹⁰ ohm⁻¹m⁻¹
